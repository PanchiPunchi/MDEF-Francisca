Designing in a State of Climate Emergency

This seminar, conducted by Andrés Colmenares, deals with raising awareness about the collective, polycrisis, and fostering conversation. The main pillars of the conversation were time, honorable harvest, dialogue, and the green economy.

My main take aways

Time


While one is always in the present, the mind or concentration can often wander into the future, past, or even the current moment. Regardless of the temporality of the mind, the associated emotion can be either negative or positive. Becoming aware of this helps question oneself and fosters self-awareness to enable a change in attitude if desired.

Honorable Harvest


Speaking about respecting the balance between society and nature involves acknowledging that the act of harvesting is an exchange of energy that requires both giving and receiving, along with being aware of the action being undertaken. In order to harvest sustainably over time, it is essential to approach this activity in an honorable manner

Green economy 


Questioning the growth of the economy in tandem with the rise of pollution raises important considerations. Is it possible to achieve economic growth while simultaneously reducing CO2 levels? Where is economic growth heading? This talk aims to analyze the purpose of capitalism, the culture of sufficiency, and how individuals relate to these global issues.

Conversation


To engage in a conversation, one must listen. In empathetic conversations, it is essential to learn how to listen to others to create a space of trust, allowing individuals to express themselves regarding collective issues. Generating personal analysis on these topics is a step, and the next is to discuss and bring these issues to the table. This process helps raise awareness and fosters social change.

Activity


As a group activity in the seminar, we created posters related to climate change and went to the beach, specifically Barceloneta, with the goal of engaging in conversations with people there. We managed to initiate three main conversations. It was interesting to see that people took the time to talk and share their opinions. Initially, I felt quite uncomfortable as it's not something I usually do, but I appreciate the experience and consider it positive.

Reflections

At the beginning, my expectations were more geared towards the course being "design" focused. To my surprise, I found it to be more spiritual and introspective. I liked the way the days were distributed thematically, focusing more on relationships with others. Being able to engage in conversations and reflect on where one belongs in the current crisis was valuable. My main takeaway from the seminar was the positive feeling it left me with; it was more about becoming a better human in society than a better professional.
